## **1. Introduction — Continuation Setups vs Reversal Setups** 

Session 5 builds directly on the reversal setups (IGB/IGS) taught in Session 4. While reversal setups catch the move at the very top and bottom, continuation setups allow you to enter in the middle of an already-moving trend. This session introduces all four continuation setups: RDB1, RDB2, RDS1, and RDS2. 

## **1.1 The Train Analogy — The Core Concept** 

Balakrishna uses a powerful analogy to explain the logic of continuation setups: 

- A train departs from Mumbai heading to Bangalore (destination). 

- If you miss the train at Mumbai (the starting point — i.e., you missed the reversal setup at support), you do not need to give up. 

- There are multiple intermediate stations between Mumbai and Bangalore. You can board the same train at any intermediate station using any available vehicle to chase it. 

- As long as the train has not yet reached its destination (Bangalore = resistance for a buy trade), you can board at any station. 

- Once it reaches the destination, you stop looking for continuation buy entries and instead look for reversal sell setups. 

**Core Principle:** As long as price has not reached its destination (the opposing S/R level), you can keep finding re-entry opportunities using continuation setups. Every pullback in the middle of the trend is a potential boarding station.

## **2. RDB — R Delta Buy (Continuation Buy Setups)** 

RDB stands for Reversal Delta Buy. It is used when price has already reversed from a support level and is moving upward. You use RDB to enter or re-enter the buy trade during the upward move, before it reaches resistance. 

## **2.1 The Two RDB Candle Patterns** 

||||
|---|---|---|
|**Setup**|**Candle Colour**|**Delta Colour / Direction**|
||||
||||
|**RDB1**|GREEN (positive/bullish candle)|NEGATIVE (red delta —<br>sellers inside)|
||||
||||
|**RDB2**|RED (negative/bearish candle)|POSITIVE (green delta —<br>buyers inside)|
||||



**Shared Concept:** Both RDB1 and RDB2 represent a TRAP. In RDB1, sellers tried to push price down while buyers absorbed them — the green candle with negative delta shows sellers were trapped. In RDB2, price fell but buyers were dominant inside — bears got trapped and will be squeezed out. 

**📌 Note:** _RDB1 and RDB2 are the same as IGB1 and IGB2 from Session 4 (reversal setups) in terms of their candle + delta combination. The DIFFERENCE is the LOCATION. IGB is used AT support (reversal). RDB is used IN THE MIDDLE of the upward move (continuation)._ 

## **2.2 The Six Mandatory Conditions for RDB** 

ALL six conditions must be satisfied before taking any RDB trade. Skipping even one condition invalidates the setup. 

## **RDB — All Six Conditions (ALL Must Be Met)** 

1. Price must have ALREADY reversed from a MAJOR support level. The reversal is confirmed. You are not entering at support — you are entering mid-trend after the reversal. 

2. A valid setup candle must form: either an RDB1 (green candle + negative delta) or RDB2 (red candle + positive delta). 

3. The setup candle must NOT be an Inside Bar. The entire body of the setup candle must NOT be contained within the range of the preceding candle. If it is inside, it is part of an NRC — skip it. 

4. The LOW of the setup candle must NOT be broken before the HIGH is broken. If the low breaks first → setup becomes INVALID immediately. Wait for the next setup. 

5. There must be NO immediate resistance directly above the setup candle. There must be sufficient clear space between the setup candle and the next resistance level for the trade to reach at least a 1:1 target.

6. The entry triggers ONLY when the HIGH of the setup candle is broken. Do not enter early. Mark the high and wait for price to cross it.

**📌  Rule: If the LOW of the setup candle breaks BEFORE the HIGH, the setup is INVALID — not just a stop loss hit. There is no entry. You were never in the trade. Discard the setup and wait for the next one to form.** 

## **2.4 The Inside Bar Rule — Detailed** 

The inside bar rule is critical and frequently misunderstood. Here is the exact definition: 

- An Inside Bar occurs when the ENTIRE body AND wicks of a candle are contained within the high-to-low range of the preceding candle (the Mother Candle). 

- If even a small part of the body or wick extends outside the Mother Candle's range, it is NOT an inside bar — it is a valid, independent candle. 

- Why we reject inside bars: an inside bar means the candle is part of an NRC (Narrow Range Consolidation). We never trade inside the NRC. 

- The question is binary: is the entire candle contained within the previous candle? If YES → inside bar → skip. If NO → valid candle → proceed to check other conditions. 

**📌 Note:** _There is no percentage threshold for the inside bar rule. It is all-or-nothing: if the full candle (including wicks) is inside the Mother Candle, it is an inside bar. Even 1 pip outside = NOT an inside bar._

## **4.1 When to Stop Taking RDB/RDS Trades** 

- For RDB: Stop looking for RDB entries when price is approaching or near resistance. Near the resistance, look for IGS (reversal sell setup) instead. 

- For RDS: Stop looking for RDS entries when price is approaching or near support. Near the support, look for IGB (reversal buy setup) instead. 

- The continuation setup is only valid in the MIDDLE of the journey. Once you are close to the destination, switch to reversal setups.

## **5. Trailing Stop Loss Using RDB/RDS** 

One of the most powerful applications of continuation setups is trailing the stop loss on an existing trade. This allows you to let profits run while progressively reducing risk as price moves in your favour. 

## **5.1 How Trailing Works** 

- Step 1: You take a buy trade using either an IGB (reversal) or an earlier RDB setup. Price moves upward. 

- Step 2: Price makes a small pullback and forms a new RDB setup candle (e.g., a red candle with positive delta — RDB2). 

- Step 3: This new RDB candle's LOW becomes your new stop loss level (with buffer). 

- Step 4: Trail (move) your existing stop loss to below the new RDB candle's low. 

- Step 5: Your stop loss is now closer to the current price, locking in more of your profit. 

- Step 6: As each new RDB setup forms on the way up, repeat — keep trailing the stop loss to below each new setup candle's low. 

- Step 7: Eventually, either the stop loss is hit (at a small profit or breakeven, much better than the original stop) or price reaches the target. 

**The Power of Trailing:** By trailing with each RDB, you progressively move your stop loss from below the original reversal candle (perhaps 50 points below entry) to just a few points below the most recent pullback. You capture most of the move while ensuring you exit cleanly if the trend reverses. 

## **5.2 Three Uses of Continuation Setups**

|||
|||
|**Fresh Entry (missed the**<br>**reversal)**|Missed the IGB at support? Use RDB mid-trend for a fresh entry.<br>Stop loss is tighter, risk is smaller than at the reversal.|
|||

## **6. Target Setting — How to Use NRC as a Target** 

Many students ask: where do I set my target? Balakrishna provides a clear, rules-based answer using NRC zones as natural price stopping points. 

## **6.1 Default Target — 1:1 Risk-Reward** 

- The minimum target for any trade is 1:1 risk-reward ratio. 

- If your stop loss is 20 points below entry, your minimum target is 20 points above entry. 

- If your stop loss is 50 points, minimum target is 50 points profit. 

- This ensures that even a 50% win rate produces a net positive result over time. 

## **6.2 Using NRC Zones as Targets** 

NRC zones create natural stopping points for price movement. They act like a cushion that slows and reverses price. This makes them ideal target levels. 

- For a BUY trade (RDB): Look for NRC zones ABOVE the current price. The LOW of the NRC zone (the bottom of the NRC box) is the target. Price may enter the NRC but will slow significantly. 

- For a SELL trade (RDS): Look for NRC zones BELOW the current price. The HIGH of the NRC zone (the top of the NRC box) is the target. Price approaching from above will slow at the NRC top. 

- The NRC is like a thick liquid zone. Even if price enters the NRC, it will slow and may not blast through immediately. Take profits at the NRC boundary. 

- If the NRC is the boundary of an existing support/resistance zone, it becomes an even stronger target. 

**Target Rule:** Mark the high and low of every NRC you see between your entry and the destination S/R level. These are your waypoints — take partial profits or exit at each NRC boundary. 

## **6.3 What Happens When Target is Reached** 

- When price reaches the resistance (for a buy trade) or support (for a sell trade), your continuation setups stop. 

- Switch from looking for RDB to looking for IGS (sell reversal). The trend is near its destination. 

- Switch from looking for RDS to looking for IGB (buy reversal). The trend is near its destination. 

- Never chase continuation setups near the destination — you will not get enough room for the target.

## **7. Exhaustion Candle — Recognising and Avoiding the Trap** 

An Exhaustion Candle is one of the most important warning signs in trading. It signals that the last participants in a move have rushed in — and the move is about to reverse or pause sharply. 

## **7.1 What is an Exhaustion Candle?** 

- An Exhaustion Candle is a large, fast-moving candle that covers an unusually wide price range in a single session or short period. 

- It typically appears at the END of a trend — when the last buyers (in an uptrend) or last sellers (in a downtrend) pile in emotionally. 

- The candle opens and moves very far in one direction without a normal pullback — it looks like a 'spike' or 'blast' candle. 

- It signals exhaustion of the participants: all the willing buyers (or sellers) have entered. There is no one left to push price further. A reversal or pause is imminent. 

## **7.2 Recognising an Exhaustion Candle** 

- The candle is significantly larger than the average candle size around it. 

- The opening is far from the previous close — often a gap up or gap down. 

- The candle body is very large, with relatively small wicks. 

- Volume (if visible) is unusually high on this candle. 

- The candle appears at the end of a trend, not in the middle. 

**Real Example:** During a bullish run, gold opened high with a massive gap-up candle covering thousands of points. This was the exhaustion candle — the last buyers rushed in. Shortly after, the reversal began. Those who bought into the exhaustion candle suffered losses.

- An exhaustion candle often has very high volume (large delta number) — confirming that a large number of emotional participants entered.

- After the exhaustion, a small delta on subsequent candles confirms the excitement is fading.

## **9. Complete Trading Workflow — Step by Step** 

Putting everything together, here is the complete decision-making workflow for using continuation setups in a real trading session. 

## **9.1 For a BUY Continuation Trade (RDB)** 

- Step 1: Draw your major support and resistance levels on the 15-minute chart. 

- Step 2: Confirm that price has reversed from a major support (either through an IGB setup or clearly observed reversal). 

- Step 3: On the 5-minute chart, watch for pullbacks during the upward move. 

- Step 4: When a pullback occurs, look for an RDB1 (green candle, red delta) or RDB2 (red candle, green delta) candle. 

- Step 5: Check ALL SIX conditions before considering entry. 

- Step 6: Mark the HIGH and LOW of the setup candle on the chart. 

- Step 7: Place a buy stop order ABOVE the HIGH (with buffer). Wait. 

- Step 8: If LOW breaks first → cancel the order, setup is invalid. Wait for next. 

- Step 9: If HIGH breaks → entry triggers. Immediately place stop loss BELOW the LOW (with buffer). 

- Step 10: Set target at the nearest NRC zone above, or resistance level, minimum 1:1 R:R. 

- Step 11: If another RDB forms before target is hit → trail stop loss to below the new setup candle's low. 

- Step 12: Stop looking for RDB when price is close to resistance. Switch to IGS watch. 

## **9.2 For a SELL Continuation Trade (RDS)** 

- Step 1: Draw your major support and resistance levels on the 15-minute chart. 

- Step 2: Confirm that price has reversed from a major resistance (either through an IGS setup or clearly observed reversal). 

- Step 3: On the 5-minute chart, watch for pullbacks (upward bounces) during the downward move. 

- Step 4: When a pullback/bounce occurs, look for an RDS1 (red candle, green delta) or RDS2 (green candle, red delta) candle. 

- Step 5: Check ALL SIX conditions before considering entry. 

- Step 6: Mark the HIGH and LOW of the setup candle on the chart.

## **11. Session 5 Golden Rules** 

- **# Golden Rule 1** RDB and RDS are continuation setups. They are used to enter in the MIDDLE of a trend, not at the top or bottom. 

- **2** The train analogy: if you miss the reversal at support/resistance, you can board the same trend at any intermediate pullback using RDB/RDS. 

- **3** RDB is for buy continuation after a reversal from support. RDS is for sell continuation after a reversal from resistance. 

- **4** RDB1 = Green candle + Negative delta. RDB2 = Red candle + Positive delta. **5** RDS1 = Red candle + Positive delta. RDS2 = Green candle + Negative delta. **6** ALL SIX conditions must be met. A setup that fails even one condition is not a valid trade. **7** The inside bar rule: if the ENTIRE setup candle (including wicks) is inside the prior candle, skip it. It is part of an NRC. 

- **8** For RDB: if the LOW breaks before the HIGH — the setup is INVALID. Cancel the pending order immediately. 

- **9** For RDS: if the HIGH breaks before the LOW — the setup is INVALID. Cancel the pending order immediately. 

- **10** There must be no immediate resistance (RDB) or support (RDS) ahead. If there is no room for a 1:1 target, do not take the trade. 

- **11** Entry for RDB: only when the HIGH breaks. Entry for RDS: only when the LOW breaks. Never enter early. 

- **12** Stop loss is placed AFTER entry triggers — never before. RDB: stop below setup candle low + buffer. RDS: stop above setup candle high + buffer. 

- **13** Default target: minimum 1:1 risk-reward ratio. Always. **14** NRC zones in the path of the trade act as natural targets. Use the NRC low (for RDB) or NRC high (for RDS) as the target level. 

- **15** Stop looking for RDB when price is near resistance. Stop looking for RDS when price is near support. Switch to reversal setup watch. 

- **16** Trailing stop loss: each new RDB/RDS that forms on the way to target allows you to move your stop loss to below/above the new setup candle. 

- **17** An Exhaustion Candle is a large, fast candle at the end of a trend — it means all participants have entered. Do NOT trade into it. Wait for the next structure. 

- **18** Bearish markets are easier to trade than bullish markets. Fewer gap-downs, more predictable moves, more RDS opportunities. 

- **19** These setups are 100% intraday. Do NOT hold RDB or RDS trades overnight.

## **Core Practice Tasks** 

- On any 5-minute chart (Nifty, Bank Nifty, Crude Oil), identify three complete upward moves from a major support to resistance. On each move, mark every RDB1 and RDB2 candle that formed during pullbacks. Verify all six conditions for each. 

- Similarly, find three complete downward moves and mark all RDS1 and RDS2 candles. Verify conditions. 

- Practice the invalidation rule: on each setup you mark, check — did the invalidation side break first? How many setups would have been invalidated before entry? This builds discipline. 

- Practice the inside bar rule: on any chart, find five inside bars. For each, identify the Mother Candle that contains them. Confirm you would have skipped these as NRC candles. 

- Use the backtest/replay feature on Pro Charting: go back 2-3 weeks, run the chart forward in real time, and practice calling RDB/RDS setups live as the candles form. Mark entry, stop loss, and target. 

- Practice trailing stop loss: on a backtested upward move, trail your stop loss below each new RDB candle that forms. Calculate how much profit you would have locked in at each step. 

- Identify three exhaustion candles on historical charts. Note what happened after each one — did price reverse? How quickly? 

- On Bank Nifty or Crude Oil charts, practice the RDS exclusively for one full week of backtesting. RDS in a downtrend is particularly reliable.
